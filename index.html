About

The F1 Bingo Card Generator is a tool to help you generate a bingo card for the 2023 Formula 1 season in a matter of seconds. Every bingo card that is generated is in a random order, so you can play with your friends and family by asking them to generate a card as well. The generators bingo cards are designed to be used with the rules below, but you can use it however you want. This tool was created because one of our developers was making F1 bingo cards for his friends and family, and he thought it would be a good idea to make a tool to help others do the same. Please visit the github page if you like our tool, github stars are a great way to show your support. We also have a discord server, where you can ask questions or give feedback.

About the Generator

How does it generate the bingo cards?

The bingo card generator always puts the race starts square in the middle of the card. It then places the safety car, vsc, and red flag squares randomly around the card. There are 72 other possible squares that are be placed at random in the remaining 21 squares. The generator will never place two of the same square in the same bingo card. This means that if you generate more than one bingo card, it is highly unlikely that you will get the card.

Intended Rules

The intention with the squares that have the circles with red numbers in them is that the event on the square has to happen that many times for the square to be marked. For example, the race starts square has 23 circles in it, a race has to start 23 times for the square to be marked. After each race start you cross off one number to keep track of how many times the event has happened. If you cross off all the numbers, you can mark the square.

Other than the above normal bingo rules apply.

...but saying all that you can use the cards however you want, just have fun!

See below for a list of all the possible squares and when the creators think they should be checked off.

Possible Squares

On every bingo card you will find the following squares:

Race Starts

This square appears at the center of every bingo card. It has 23 race starts that must be crossed off before you can cross off the bingo square. It's upto you wether you choose to count race restarts as race starts.

Safety Car

This square appears in every bingo card, but in a random location. The intention is for the numbers to be crossed off when the safety car goes on track until you reach 10 safety cars, then cross off the square. The Virtual Safety Car does not count towards this square.

Virtual Safety Car

This square appears in every bingo card, but in a random location. The intention is for the numbers to be crossed off when the virtual safety car takes effect on track until you reach 8 virtual safety cars, then cross off the square. The regular Safety Car does not count towards this square.

Red Flag

This square appears in every bingo card, but in a random location. The intention is for the numbers to be crossed off when the red flag is shown on track until you reach 4 red flags, then cross off the square.

On every bingo card you will find a random 21 of the following squares:

Max has won the WDC

When Max (inevitably) wins the 2023 World Drivers' Championship.

First time winner

The winner of a race did not have a race win in Formula 1 before that race. It is upto you if you count sprint race wins for this square.

No Red Bull, Mercedes, or Ferrari appears on the podium

No Red Bull, Mercedes, or Ferrari appears on the podium. Everyone loves a good underdog story! Especially when it's everyone on the podium.

Lando Norris wins a race

When Lando Norris gets his first win in Formula 1. It's only a matter of time, right guys? Right? It's upto you if a sprint race win counts for this square.

First lap crash

There is a crash during the first lap of a race. Two are required for this square.

Lewis has won the WDC

When Lewis wins the 2023 World Drivers' Championship. This ones a bit of a long shot this year, but it's here for the die hard Lewis fans.

Wet Race

When rain falls during a race. Two are required for this square. It's upto you if rain during a sprint race counts for this square.

Wet Qualifying

When rain falls during qualifying. Two are required for this square.

First time pole

P1 during a qualifying session is the first time a driver has ever been on pole. It's upto you if you count a first pole from a sprint race for this square.

Any team gets a 1-2 finish

Both drivers for a single constructor finish P1 and P2 in the same race. This has to happen two times for this square. It is upto you if you count a 1-2 in a sprint race for this square.

Rookie Points

When the F1 2023 rookies score their first points in Formula 1, check off the circle with the rookie's number. Once all 3 rookies have scored points, check off the square.

Reserve Driver Drives

When a reserve driver replace a driver on the grid for a race weekend.

Ferrari wins in Italy

Ferrari wins at any of the races in Italy. Lets's hope they can at least nail the strategy at one race.

A pit stop takes longer than twenty seconds

A pit stop takes longer than twenty seconds. This is counting time in the pit box, not the pit lane. It doesn't count if the driver retires during the pit stop.

All teams have scored points

When all teams have scored points in a season. This is counting the constructors championship, not the drivers championship.

All drivers have scored points

When all drivers have scored points in a season. This is counting the drivers championship, not the constructors championship.

Zero to Hero

A driver goes from a non-points scoring grid position, to P1 during a race. For a regular race this is P11 to P1. If you choose to count a sprint race for this card it would be P9 to P1.

Penalty given out mid-race

When the punishment for a penalty is given out during the race. A penalty given out because of an event in free practice or qualifying does not count. A penalty given out post-race does not count to this square.

Fire!

Anything is visibly on fire on the track or in the pit lane. Up there with the most scary things in F1.

Rookie podium

A 2023 rookie driver finishes on the podium. It is upto you if sprint races count for this square.

Zero DNF race

There are no retirements from a race. Sprint races do not count for this square.

Greater than 5 DNFs

There are more than 5 DNFs in one race. It is upto you if sprint races count for this square.

A driver ignores team orders

When a driver is told to help their team mate, but they ignore the order. Don't worry the commentary team will let you know when this one happens.

Speeding in the pit lane

When someone is caught speeding in the pit lane.

Post race penalty

When a penalty is given out to a driver after a race. After qualifying or free practice does not count. Penalties given during the race do not count for this square.

Carlos Sainz wins a race

When Carlos Sainz wins a race during the 2023 F1 season.

Double DNF

When both teammates from one constructor retire from the same race. This way the team definitely doesn't score any points.

Williams double points

When both Williams drivers score points in the same race. This one is a bit of a long shot, but it would be nice to see.

Team crash

When two teammates from the same team crash into each other, and at least one of the gets damaged.

Haas double points

When both Haas drivers score points in the same race. This one is a bit of a long shot, but it would be nice to see.

Alex Albon dyes his hair

When Alex Albon dyes his hair a different color from what it was at the start of the season (Blonde).

Victories from pole

When the winner of a race is the driver who started on pole position. Sprint races do not count for this square. This must happen 7 times during the season for this square to be crossed off.

Someone swears on-air

When someone gets caught swearing on a live broadcast. This is usually in the cool down room after the race. The best way to tell if this happened is the commentary team will usually apologize for the language.

A Mercedes takes out a Red Bull br rear tire

When a Mercedes driver takes out a Red Bull driver by hitting their rear tire. This is a very specific square, but it does happen way to often.

Crash with greater than 3 cars

When there is a crash involving more than 3 cars. Up there with the most scary moments in F1.

Front Wing Change

When a front wing gets changed in the pits. This has to happen 5 times during the season for this square to be crossed off.

Driver Retires

When a driver on the grid in 2023 announces their retirement from F1.

Seven different race winners

When seven different drivers have won a race during the season. It is upto you if sprint races count for this square.

Bono! My tires are gone!

When Lewis Hamilton complains to his engineer that his tires are gone, but then proceeds to set the fastest lap on those same tires.

Home win

When any driver wins a race in their home country. It is upto you if sprint races count for this square.

All British podium

When all three podium positions after a race are filled by British drivers. It is upto you if sprint races count for this square.

Daniel Ricciardo participates in a race

When Daniel Ricciardo steps in a reserve driver and takes part in a race weekend.

Rookie crash

When any of the 2023 rookies crash their car. This has to happen 6 times for this square to be crossed off.

Someone locked up

When any driver locks up a wheel under braking. This has to happen 15 times for this square to be crossed off.

Aussie podium

When any Australian driver finishes on the podium. It is upto you if sprint races count towards this square.

All commonwealth podium

When all the drivers on the podium are from a country in the commonwealth. It is upto you if sprint races count towards this square.

Race ends because of the time limit

When a race ends because of the time limit, and not because all of the laps have been completed.

Toto smashes a table

When the broadcast shows Toto Wolff smashing a table in anger.

Celerity spotted

When the broadcast shows a celerity in the pits during a race. This has to happen 8 times for this square to be crossed off.

A type of wet and slick tire are used in the same race

When in the span of one race both a type of wet and slick tire are used. Sprint races do count towards this square.

A qualifying position is lost becuase of track limits

When a qualifying position is lost because the driver went outside the track limits. This doesn't count if the position is not lost.

Silly Season begins

When a driver signs with a new team that they were not part of before, or when a team decares they are looking for a new driver for the next season.

Pole by crashing

When a driver crashes in qualifying and still gets pole.

Valtteri podiums with his mullet

When Valtteri Bottas podiums with his mullet. it is upto you if sprint races count towards this square.

Hulkenberg podium

When Nico Hulkenberg gets a podium in the F1 2023 season.

Perez outqualifying Verstappen

When Sergio Perez outqualifies Max Verstappen in the F1 2023 season.

Fake water present

When there is fake water present at a race weekend in the F1 2023 season, like in Miami 2022.

Polotical penalty

When a driver gets a penalty because of the FIA's new anti-political message stance.

Ferrari Fail

When Ferrari loses a good race result becuase they made an outragously bad strategy call.

New WDC or WCC

When a new driver wins the World Drivers' Championship for the first time or a new constructor wins World Constructors' Championship in the F1 2023 season. For the WCC we will count new as starting in the turbo-hybrid era.

A race gets canceled

When a race gets canceled in the F1 2023 season, for any reason.

Will the race happen?

When the press speculates if the race will get canceled or not. This would be because of the polotical state of the county the race is taking place in

Max's teammate changes for next season

When Max Verstappen's teammate changes for the next season. This would be for the 2024 season. It's called the hotseat for a reason.

Limp to a win

A driver wins a race with significant damage to their car.

Vettel races

When Vettel steps in a reserve driver and takes part in a race weekend.

Equal Machinery

When Nico Rosberg mentions that he beat Lewis Hamilton in equal machinery.

Brundle corrects Crofty

When Martin Brundle corrects Crofty on a mistake he made during the broadcast.

Driver lashes out

When a driver lashes out because of a how m=bad their car is

Stroll crashes

When Lance Stroll crashes his car while being passed or while passing another car. Usually becuase he didn't look in his mirrors enough.

Fernando loses a podium

When Fernando Alonso loses a podium because of reliability issues.

Gasly v. Piastri

When Pierre Gasly beats Oscar Piastri in the World Drivers' Championship.
